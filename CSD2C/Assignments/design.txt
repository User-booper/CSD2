Knitted stretch sensors are made of a knit fabric of conductive yarns, or alternatively from a non-conductive knit fabric coated in a conductive polymer. As they stretch they change their resistance because the stretching creates more contact points between the interlocked loops (stitches). In the above picture the stretch sensor is knitted from stainless steel and polyester yarn.
